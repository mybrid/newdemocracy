This book is divided into two independent sections where each chapter has the section included:

1. Overview
2. Handbook

Irreni Society is new. It is a Mesh Democracy design as opposed to a Republic. The Overview section gives insight and intuition as to the why and motivation of Mesh Democracy. However, if one is at their wits end with current Democracy and ready to just dig in then jump to the Handbook section.

The Overview section is in a schematic or sketch format. There are no citations. Partly due to the fact that I'm lazy. I've been working on Irreni since 1980 and haven't kept any notes except my own thoughts. Citations will have to be the effort of other people when the time comes. This is one reason the Overview section addresses just why and motivation. This book then fits within the tradition of Thomas Payne's, "The Rights of Man". Discussing the academic reasons for why and motivation doesn't need to be precise with citations. The objective of the Overview section is to scratch an itch. That itch is curiosity for answers:

1. What is Mesh Democracy?
2. How does it compare to current Democracy?

The Handbook section is what one would expect, how to do this-and-that. Irreni Society is a social engineering project. It is not an ideology project.  As engineering then various parts and pieces can be used independent of the whole. Even if one disagrees with much of the design and engineering of Irreni, then one can still create something new and better using various parts one finds agreeable.

My first handbook was a book of Math tables. I'm old enough to have used slide rulers. However, slide rules were not stand-alone technology. Math handbooks with tables of precalculated commonly used numbers existed long before the slide rule. I used this until Sophomore year in high school. Then I bought a scientific calculator from HP.

My next handbook was, "The Runner's Handbook". This book still exists and has advice for day-to-day training, marathon training, common injuries, and so on.

The Handbook section is in the spirit of these kinds of handbooks but not in body. The  Handbook section is a handbook of quests with suggestions on going about them. You are adventurer going on a quest. One is going on a quest like with Frodo taking the ring to Mount Doom. The initial quest is to find fellow adventurers needed, thirty people to form an Mesh Governing Organization, or MGO. The MGO is the mesh node in Mesh Democracy. How one accomplishes the quest is up to the adventurer where suggestions are given. The Handbook continues assigning finer grained quests with suggestions. The quests are all in support of establishing the MGO.

The Handbook section omits projects. Projects are how two-or-more MGOs coordinate to engineer projects greater than the capabilities of a single MGO. If one is curious about projects there are explanations in the Overview section.

Projects are not included in the Handbook section because forming an MGO will take years. Currently real estate is built as single-family dwellings or apartments. We need new real estate built for thirty people. Further the campus needs new buildings such as a medical bay, dental bay, stores for a year's supply of food and water, and manufacturing. These campuses do not exist today. Therefore the initial generation will focus on getting MGO campuses off the ground.
